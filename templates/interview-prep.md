## Product Sense Framework (CIRCLES-Lite)

1. **Clarify** — What type of product? Who is the customer? What platform?
2. **Users** — Who are the user segments? Pick 1 to focus on.
3. **Pain Points** — What are the top 3 problems for that user?
4. **Solutions** — Generate 5+ solutions without filtering. Then filter.
5. **Prioritize** — Impact × Effort × Strategic fit. Pick top 2.
6. **Trade-offs** — What do you give up? What are the risks?
7. **Metrics** — How do you measure success? What are guardrails?

---

## Metric Drop Diagnosis Framework

When a metric drops, always go in this order:

```
1. External factors — outages, seasonality, news, competitors
2. Data / logging issues — is the measurement broken?
3. Segment by: platform, geo, user type, feature, time of day
4. Recent releases — what shipped in the last 7 days?
5. Funnel analysis — where in the funnel did it drop?
6. Form your hypothesis — most likely cause
7. Proposed fix — what do you do next?
```

**Never** jump to "we should fix the UI" before diagnosing.

## Common Entry-Level PM Questions + Best Answers

### "Why do you want to be a PM?"
**Weak:** "I like working with teams and technology."  
**Strong:** "I've always operated at the intersection of operations, users, and systems. When I built [Drivn/Hangman], I realized what I loved most wasn't writing the code — it was figuring out *what* to build and why. I've been doing PM work informally; I want to do it officially."

### "You don't have PM experience — why should we hire you?"
**Weak:** "I'm a fast learner and very motivated."  
**Strong:** "I don't have the title, but I have the thinking. I've owned product decisions on [project], run user discovery, written PRDs, prioritized features with limited resources, and shipped real software. I also have an ops background, which most PMs lack — I understand execution, not just strategy."

### "How do you handle ambiguity?"
**Weak:** "I just try to figure it out."  
**Strong:** "I break it into: what do I know, what do I need to know, and who can help me know it. Then I make the best decision I can with current data, document my reasoning, and stay open to updating it. I've done this in [specific situation]."

### "Tell me about a product you love and why."
**Template:** "[Product] is one of the most well-designed [category] tools because it optimizes for [job to be done] better than anyone. Specifically, [feature X] does something most people overlook: [insight]. If I were a PM there, I'd focus on [improvement area] because [strategic reason]."

## Mock Interview Protocol

1. Set a 25-minute timer
2. Read the question out loud
3. Take 30 seconds to structure your answer (jot notes)
4. Answer out loud — record yourself
5. Review: Did you clarify first? Use data? Stay structured?
6. Grade yourself: Clarity (1–5) · Structure (1–5) · Insight (1–5)
7. Re-answer if score < 12/15

**Do this 3x/week minimum during active interview stage.**
