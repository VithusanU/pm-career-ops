# PM Resume System

## Core Principle
Every bullet = **[Strong Action Verb] + [What You Did] + [Quantified Result]**  
Never describe duties. Describe outcomes.

---

## Resume Structure

```
[Full Name]
[Email] · [LinkedIn] · [Portfolio URL] · [GitHub]

Execution-focused PM candidate with background in operations leadership and 
software development. Built [Projects]. Strong product thinking, analytics 
mindset, and track record of shipping systems that reduce friction and drive 
measurable outcomes.

PROJECTS (lead with this if no PM experience yet)
```

## ATS Keyword Bank (PM Roles)

### Must-have keywords
- Product roadmap
- Cross-functional collaboration
- User research / discovery
- A/B testing / experimentation
- Data-driven decision making
- Prioritization (RICE, ICE, MoSCoW)
- Stakeholder management
- Go-to-market (GTM)
- KPIs / OKRs / metrics
- MVP / iterative development
- Sprint planning / agile / scrum
- Product requirements document (PRD)

### Nice-to-have (industry-specific)
- Growth / retention / engagement
- Conversion rate optimization
- Funnel analysis / cohort analysis
- Customer journey mapping
- Feature flag / rollout
- SQL / data analysis
- API / technical requirements

## Customization Checklist (per application)

- [ ] Read the full JD — highlight recurring keywords (3+ appearances = critical)
- [ ] Check if they say "data-driven" — add a metrics-focused bullet
- [ ] Check if they say "cross-functional" — ensure you have a bullet showing it
- [ ] Match their language (they say "roadmap" → you say "roadmap", not "plan")
- [ ] Adjust summary to mirror their company mission language
- [ ] Top 3 bullets should address top 3 requirements of the JD
- [ ] Remove anything irrelevant to PM — keep resume 1 page

## Cover Letter Framework

**Para 1 — The Hook (2–3 sentences)**  
Why THIS company, not "a company." Reference a specific product decision, launch, or strategy.  
*"I've been using [Product] since [X] and recently noticed [specific observation]. The way your team handled [thing] showed [insight]. That's why I want to be a PM here."*

**Para 2 — Your Proof (3–4 sentences)**  
Connect your most relevant experience to what they need.  
*"In my [role], I [achievement + metric]. When I built [project], I [PM-relevant action]. These experiences taught me [insight relevant to their problems]."*

**Para 3 — Why You Fit (2–3 sentences)**  
Connect your values/style to their culture.  
*"[Company] values [thing from their careers page]. I [evidence you embody this]. I'd bring [specific contribution] to your PM team."*

**Para 4 — Close (2 sentences)**  
Confident, not desperate.  
*"I'd love to discuss how I can contribute to [specific product area]. Thank you for your time — I look forward to connecting."*

## Fast-Apply vs. Tailored Decision

| Situation | Approach |
|-----------|----------|
| Dream company, strong fit | Full tailored (45 min) |
| Good fit, tier 1 company | Tailored bullets + custom cover letter (20 min) |
| Decent fit, volume play | ATS keyword swap, generic cover letter (10 min) |
| Stretch role | Skip or fast-apply only |

**Rule:** 3 tailored/week, 7 fast-apply/week = 10 total. Quality gets interviews. Volume creates optionality.
